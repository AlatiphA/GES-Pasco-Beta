Frequently Asked Questions

Getting Started
GES Pasco is a free offline EPUB reader for Ghana Education Service (GES) workers preparing for promotion aptitude exams. It contains past questions and answers, and footnotes.
Visit the app link in your browser and wait for the install prompt, then tap Install. If the prompt doesn't appear, tap the 3 dots in your browser and select Add to Home Screen. On iPhone, tap the Share button then Add to Home Screen.
Yes! Once installed and opened at least once with internet, the book and the app itself load fully offline.
Reading
Tap the menu button on the bottom left corner of the reader to open the sidebar, tap on contents tab and select the chapter (section).
Tap left or right on the reading area, or use the arrow buttons at the bottom of the screen.
Use the A− and A+ buttons in the bottom toolbar to decrease or increase the font size.
Tap the magnifying glass icon (🔍) in the bottom toolbar and type your search term. Results will appear as a list — tap any result to jump to that location.
Themes & Bookmarks
Tap the palette icon (🎨) in the top right of the reader to choose between Light, Dark, Sepia, and Night themes.
Tap the bookmark icon (🔖) in the bottom toolbar while reading. Your bookmark is saved automatically with the chapter (section) name and progress.
Tap the menu icon at the bottom of the reader, then tap the Bookmarks tab in the sidebar. The bookmarks will be listed with a delete icons by them. A tap on the delete icon (🗑), deletes the bookmark by it.
Troubleshooting
Make sure you opened the app at least once with internet so books can be cached. If the problem persists, tap the trash icon (🗑) in the top left of the reader header to clear app data, then reopen with internet on.
It resets all app data including bookmarks, themes, and cached files. Use it only if the app is misbehaving. You will need internet to reload the books after clearing.
Close the app completely and reopen it. If it still doesn't work, tap the trash icon (🗑) in the top left of the reader header, then reopen with internet on.
